Why Now

For decades, access to sensitive data has blocked scientific and societal progress. Today, with mature PETs and attribution-based infrastructure, we finally have a way to solve this problem.

The Barrier Era

Data stays siloed. Research slows. Yet, for a good reason: risks outweighed reward, from privacy, legal, IP and ethical concerns.

The Missed Opportunity

Researchers were limited to public data — missing critical insights and slowing discovery. Now, the public faces a similar crisis: AI systems increasingly shape what people see, decide, and believe. However, when they’re wrong, no one knows it.

The data that could produce better answers already exists, sitting on researchers’ and institutions’ machines.

The Unlock Moment

Now: With PETs and attribution-based access matured, we can unlock those critical datasets. This isn’t just more data — it’s orders of magnitudes more better data. Real-world, permissioned sources that were never shareable before can now inform AI.

The result? More accurate, state-of-the-art models, faster scientific discovery, and an economic model that rewards institutions which produced this invaluable data.
