Missing bit: Attribution-based control

In today's AI workflows, contributions flow in ‚Äî but intent doesn't.

There's no built-in way for data or model owners to say: "This is what my asset is for. This is how it should (or shouldn't) be used." and for users to ask: "Who contributed to this result, and do I trust them?".

Attribution-based control is the missing, shared mechanism.

Without this, we see familiar failures: biased results, misinformation, misuse ‚Äî not because AI is inherently harmful, but because it lacks the ability to coordinate intent across distributed actors. This, however, is a solvable problem.
